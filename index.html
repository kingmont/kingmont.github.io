Property Assessment Overview

Market Value Assessment:

A market value based assessment does NOT necessarily identify a property's individual sale price or potential sale price. Rather, it attempts to provide a ‘fair actual’ or average value based on several factors including similar properties which have sold.

Further, your current assessment notice does not reflect today's market trends; it is instead based upon market conditions one year prior to today. Any changes (increases or decreases) in property values since then will NOT be reflected on the current assessment. See ‘valuation date’ below.

Valuation

Legislation requires the assessment process to comply with the “valuation date”

Valuation date

The valuation date ensures all properties are valued as of the same date. The valuation date or the 2011 tax year is July 1, 2010. This means the assessment value on the 2011 Assessment and Tax Notice is an estimate of value as of July 1, 2010.

Appealing your assessment

You cannot appeal your property taxes, nor can you request a review of your property taxes. If you feel an error has been made, you can appeal the property assessment value (there may be an appeal fee involved). You must explain why you think your assessment is incorrect and you must be prepared to provide supporting documentation and evidence to support your claim. In other words it is insufficient to simply state “my assessment is too high”.

Contact

If you have questions or concerns about the assessment process or the value that the assessor has placed on your property, you can contact your municipality who will in turn relay the message to the assessor.
